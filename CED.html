instrument to bring an end to ignorance and curb poverty. To promote education and
extend opportunity to a great number of youth in the city, the CGC Scholarship Program
was created to provide young Calapeños with free access to quality tertiary education to

2. CITY MUSEUM SERVICES

   The City Museum offers free tour to interested clients and features the
photographs of the city’s evolution from being a simple village in the pre-Spanish era
to being a vibrant city in the 21st century. Also, adding to the number of items displaying
the impressive history of the city and the country, the museum also exhibits donated
pieces such as Philippine coins as well as clothes and various items owned by the past
leaders of Calapan.

4. ESTABLISHMENT OF BARANGAY READING CENTERS

Pursuant to RA 7743 or “An Act Providing for the Establishment of Congressional,
City and Municipal Libraries and Barangay Reading Centers throughout the Philippines,
Appropriating Necessary Funds Thereof,” Barangay Reading Centers were established
to make a wide array of reading materials including barangay history and approved
ordinances available to the community. The center may also be used as venue for
audio-visual presentations, other exhibits and activities aimed at increasing the literacy
rate in the community.

5. SUMMER ART AND BASIC READING ENHANCEMENT WORKSHOPS

The Summer Art Workshop and Basic Reading Enhancement Workshop for
children is an annual program of the City Education Department through the City
Library. The program is designed to provide free visual arts and reading skills
enhancement training and is intended for all interested Calapeňo children with ages five
to twelve years. The activities are designed to provide children with productive ways of
spending their summer vacation as well as develop the participant’s self-confidence and
creativity.
